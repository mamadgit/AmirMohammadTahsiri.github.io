UWE Bristol, Jan 2023 – Dec 2023

Analysed and compared existing semantic structures on formally modelling agents’ knowledge and reasoning process to conclude a new perspective regarding the design of said structures.

  • Most research in this area agree to reach the computationally grounded threshold of semantic structures, the agents’ local states must be considered alongside the system’s global states. However, disagreement arises if a structure adequately captures the local states to reach this threshold.
  • This research review presents various research with different designs for their agents’ local states and by comparison, concludes that computationally grounding is not threshold-based but gradational.
  • This comparison has been shown through hypothetical scenarios (i.e., swarm of self-driving cars).
  • The theoretical and practical comparison portray a new perspective towards designing semantic structures. That is: The adequate level of computational grounding for the agents’ local states must consider their environment; certain environments may require more abstract designs.
