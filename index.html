Using eye-tracking technology for clinical studies and UX research
Shahid Beheshti Institute for Cognitive & Brain Sciences, Sep 2016 – Dec 2018

  • Conducted various experiments from researching autism to user experience using eye-tracking.
  • Designed the experiments on the eye-tracking software (SMI & Begaze 3.7).
  • Implemented innovative techniques for more precise results: gaze calibration mid-experiment in addition to the standard calibration pre-experiment.
  • Researched the attention levels of both normal and autistic children on two sets of designed images.
  • Performed statistical analysis (t-test and ANOVA) on the children’s rapid eye movements and gaze patterns obtained from four different age groups to derive meaningful results.
  • Conducted user behaviour analysis on Djikala websites (akin to Amazon) using eye-tracking to study gaze patterns (fixations and saccades) of 55 participants for enhancing user navigation.
  • Utilised methodologies, such as user interviews, surveys, and usability testing to cross-reference with gathered data to reach meaningful results.
